08Why you need project planning and tracking

If any of these sound familiar, that's what Ingantt is for.

“When will this actually be done?”

You have 100 tasks and a gut feeling. Your boss wants a number.

Critical Path Method calculates your end date from dependencies and durations — backed by math, not guesswork.

“What happens if this changes?”

A supplier slips 2 weeks. Which of your 200 downstream tasks break?

Live rescheduling cascades every change through the plan in seconds. See the impact before you say yes.

“Who's overbooked this week?”

Half your team is at 120% capacity, the other half is idle. You find out the hard way.

Team Planner and Resource Usage views surface overallocations the moment they appear — not when deadlines start slipping.

“Are we actually on track?”

Everything's "green" until the week before the deadline, then suddenly red.

Tracking Gantt shows plan vs. actual side by side. Earned Value Management gives you objective schedule and cost metrics.

“Can we just add this one small thing?”

Each tiny request seems harmless. By month's end you're three weeks behind, and nobody knows why.

Live CPM reschedule quantifies every "small change" in seconds. Say yes with the number attached.

“How much will it actually cost?”

You can price the parts. You can't price the plan — labor, overtime, materials, vendor fees, all tied to a moving schedule.

Model labor rates, fixed costs, and material consumption per task. Cost rolls up and recalculates with every schedule change.

Why this matters

Only ~31% of projects finish on time, on budget, and on scope (PMI). Organizations with a formal PM approach meet goals 73% of the time — vs. 58% without (PMI). 77% of companies use project management software (PwC).

11The four jobs of a project plan

When does it finish? Who's doing what? How much does it cost? Are we on track? Ingantt answers each one with the same depth as MS Project — in your browser.

01 — Time

Plan the time

When will this actually be done?

  • Critical Path Method computes the finish date from dependencies and durations — not guesswork.
  • 4 dependency types, 8 constraint types, split & recurring tasks, 9 work-contour types.
  • Custom calendars, work weeks, and elapsed durations.
  • Change one thing — the entire schedule recalculates instantly.
02 — Resources

Plan the resources

Who's overbooked? Who's idle?

  • Assign work, material, cost, budget, and generic resources to tasks.
  • Resource Usage and Team Planner views surface overallocation the moment it appears.
  • Automatic leveling by priority, slack, or ID — within available slack only if you say so.
  • Rate tables A–E with date-effective rates and time-varying availability.
03 — Cost

Plan the cost

How much will it actually cost?

  • Cost rolls up from resource rates, fixed costs, and material consumption.
  • Fixed-cost accrual modes — start, end, or prorated.
  • 13 Earned Value columns (BCWS, BCWP, CPI, SPI, EAC, VAC, TCPI…) with % Complete or Physical % Complete EV methods.
  • Every schedule change recalculates the cost in the same beat.
04 — Progress

Track the progress

Are we on track?

  • 11 baselines (0–10) plus 10 interim plans — keep history without losing it.
  • 55 baseline columns + 5 variance columns; baseline bars overlay the Gantt.
  • Update Project and Reschedule Uncompleted Work after the status date.
  • Progress lines on the Gantt show plan vs. actual at a glance.
Throughout

AI to draft and edit your plan. Google Drive to share. MS Project / Primavera files to import and export. No new account, no separate AI subscription.

18Frequently asked questions

What is Ingantt?

Ingantt is a Gantt chart project planning app with AI. It runs in your browser, saves to Google Drive, and offers MS Project-grade scheduling power — CPM, resource leveling, earned value, 10 views, and more. Also available on Android, iOS, Windows, and macOS.

Can I import Microsoft Project files?

Yes. Ingantt opens MPP, MPT, MPX, and XML files from MS Project, plus XER and PMXML from Primavera P6, and files from GanttProject, ProjectLibre, Asta Powerproject, and many more.

How does AI plan generation work?

Describe your project in plain words and AI generates a complete schedule with tasks, dependencies, durations, and a work breakdown structure. You can use it as-is or customize it.

Can I edit my project with Claude or ChatGPT?

Yes. Ingantt's Edit with AI feature connects your project to Claude Code, Codex, or any HTTP-capable AI assistant you already use — no extra AI subscription required. Ask for changes in plain words — "add a two-week QA phase after development" or "assign Alex to all design tasks" — and watch the Gantt chart update live. Every change is validated and the full schedule reschedules through CPM after each edit.

Is Ingantt a good alternative to Microsoft Project?

Yes. Ingantt supports the same professional scheduling features — Critical Path Method, resource leveling, earned value, 10 views, baselines — and opens MS Project files directly. The difference: it runs in your browser with no install, saves to Google Drive, and costs a fraction of the price. Many users switch from MS Project and feel right at home.

What's included in the free trial?

Everything. Full access to every feature for 7 days, no credit card required.

Where is my data stored?

Your project files live in Google Drive, under your organization's own security and sharing policies. Ingantt stores no project data on its servers.

Does it work on mobile?

Yes. Ingantt for Web adapts to your screen — desktop, tablet, or phone. Your plans live in Google Drive, so you can switch devices and pick up where you left off. Native apps are also available for Android and iOS.

Can I use Ingantt for construction scheduling?

Yes. Ingantt supports the same CPM scheduling that construction managers rely on — critical path, resource leveling, baselines, and Primavera P6 file import (XER, PMXML). Many construction teams use it as a lighter, browser-based alternative to Primavera P6 or MS Project.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

Your files stay in Google Drive — they're yours. Plans are saved in MS Project-compatible XML, so you can open them in other tools if needed. No vendor lock-in.
